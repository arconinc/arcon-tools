# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Internal employees across all departments at Arcon Solutions (a promotional products & supplies company): sales, marketing, HR, accounting, warehouse, IT, e-commerce, and general staff. They use this dashboard to manage CRM tasks, expense reports, documents, news, employee profiles, order tracking, and cross-functional workflows. Work context spans office and remote; devices include desktop/laptop primary, some tablet/mobile support needed.

## Product Purpose

The Arcon Tools dashboard is the hub for internal operations. It consolidates departmental task boards, employee directory, CRM data, expense workflows, and company updates in one place. Success means employees find what they need quickly, feel connected to the company, and can execute their work without friction. The app should feel like a thoughtful colleague: helpful, organized, and human.

## Brand Personality

**Competent, approachable, clear.** Three words: *professional, warm, efficient*. The brand carries confidence (we know how to run our business) balanced with approachability (we're not stuffy; people like working here). Purple is the signature brand color—bold and memorable without being loud. The interface should feel like Slack (friendly, clear hierarchy, strategic color use) and Figma (focused, minimal friction, design-driven) had a conversation: clean and purposeful, but with enough personality to feel human.

## Anti-references

- **SaaS generic**: the beige-cream warm-neutral body bg + all-caps eyebrow headers on every section. Too default, no personality.
- **Dark mode for its own sake**: not moody or cool; use light mode with intentional color and contrast.
- **Enterprise blue-gray corporate**: too formal, too stiff. Not the tone.
- **Flat / gray monoculture**: no depth, no hierarchy, everything feels the same weight. We have a color; use it.
- **Cluttered card grids**: rows of identical cards with icon + heading + text. Prioritize breathing room and varied hierarchies.

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity first, delight second.** The interface serves busy employees. Make the job obvious; add personality where it doesn't slow them down.
2. **Color carries meaning.** The Arcon purple is not decoration; it highlights actions, status, and priorities. Every color choice should answer "why."
3. **Hierarchy through contrast, not size alone.** Weight, color, and spacing work together. A small bold accent can outweight a large gray label.
4. **Approachable, not minimal.** Minimal can feel cold. Aim for clean with warmth: good whitespace, clear typefaces, and human language in copy.
5. **Every detail works.** Buttons, inputs, cards, status badges—all are designed, not defaulted. Consistency across surfaces builds confidence.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **Target: WCAG AA** — all interactive elements, text, and color contrasts meet AA standards as baseline.
- **Color not alone:** status and meaning conveyed via color + iconography + text, never color-only (supports colorblind users).
- **Motion respectful:** all animations check `prefers-reduced-motion`; motion enhances but does not gate content visibility.
- **Keyboard-first:** all interactions (buttons, dropdowns, modals, form controls, navigation) fully keyboard-navigable without a mouse.
